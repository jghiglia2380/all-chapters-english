Chapter 15.3: Job Application Process

Student Activity Packet
The Challenge

Alex submitted dozens of random applications with a generic resume and never heard back from employers. After learning strategic job search techniques, he identified target companies, tailored his materials using keywords from each job description, and connected with a professional at one company through a tech meetup. Within two weeks of submitting his customized application—which highlighted how his capstone project demonstrated the exact skills mentioned in the job posting—he received an interview invitation. The hiring manager mentioned that his tailored application and personal connection helped him stand out from over 200 applicants.

What's the difference between applying to many jobs randomly and taking a strategic, targeted approach to the application process?

Learning Objectives

  • Identify effective strategies for finding job opportunities across different industries.
  • Understand how to read and interpret job descriptions and requirements.
  • Develop techniques for customizing application materials for specific positions.
  • Learn best practices for navigating applicant tracking systems (ATS).
  • Recognize the importance of networking in the job search process.
Core Concepts
Term Definition
Applicant Tracking System (ATS) Software used by employers to collect, sort, and filter job applications based on keywords and qualifications.
Keywords Specific terms in job descriptions that highlight required skills, qualifications, and experience—essential for passing ATS screening.
Cover Letter A document submitted with a resume that introduces you and highlights why you're qualified for the specific position.
Networking Building professional relationships to exchange information, advice, and job opportunities.
Tailoring Customizing your resume and cover letter for each job by incorporating relevant keywords and highlighting applicable experiences.
Background: The job application process is more than just sending out resumes—it requires research, preparation, and careful attention to detail. Studies show that 70-80% of jobs are never publicly advertised, highlighting the importance of networking. Additionally, most large companies use ATS software that filters applications before a human ever sees them, making keyword optimization and proper formatting essential.
Key Statistic: Approximately 75% of applications are rejected by ATS before reaching a human reviewer. Applications with tailored keywords and proper formatting are 3x more likely to pass initial screening.
