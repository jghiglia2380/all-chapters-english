Capitalism

Complete freedom to choose any job, negotiate salary, change careers. Performance determines income. Top 20% of marketers earn $150,000+ within 10 years.

Socialism

Government assigns jobs. Salary fixed at $800/month, non-negotiable. Advancement based on seniority and political connections, not performance.

Mixed Economy

High freedom with some protections. Can negotiate salary ($50K-$85K range). Anti-discrimination laws apply. Top performers earn $120,000+ in 10 years.

Capitalism

Unrestricted investment in stocks, bonds, real estate, businesses. No government limits. High potential returns but must save aggressively (20%+) for retirement.

Socialism

Private investment illegal. No stock markets or investment options. Must save in hard assets or foreign currency. Government provides pension ($600/month).

Mixed Economy

Extensive options with tax benefits. 401(k), IRA, stocks, bonds, real estate. Social Security provides baseline. Save 10-15% for comfortable retirement.

Capitalism

Minimal safety net. Unemployment insurance for 6 months only. Must build 6-12 month emergency fund. Healthcare $6,000-$15,000 annually.

Socialism

Comprehensive but low quality. Free healthcare (long waits), free housing (basic), guaranteed food rations. No risk of homelessness or starvation.

Mixed Economy

Moderate protections. Unemployment 6-12 months, Medicaid, Social Security (~$1,800/month), Medicare at 65. Safety net prevents destitution.
