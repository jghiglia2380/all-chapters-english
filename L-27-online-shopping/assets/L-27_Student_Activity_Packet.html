The Challenge

Mike found a popular gaming console on an unfamiliar website for 40% below retail price. Excited by the deal, he purchased it without researching the site. After payment, he received only an automated email. The console never arrived, and when he tried to contact customer service, the website had disappeared. He lost $300 before his credit card company helped him dispute the charge.

What warning signs did Mike miss, and how could he have protected himself from this scam?

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the benefits and risks of online shopping compared to in-store purchases.
  • Evaluate website security features to determine if a retailer is trustworthy.
  • Apply strategies to protect personal and financial information when shopping online.
Core Concepts
TermDefinition
HTTPSSecure website protocol; look for "https://" and a padlock icon in the address bar.
PhishingFraudulent attempts to steal information by impersonating legitimate businesses.
Digital WalletAn electronic service (Apple Pay, PayPal) that adds a security layer to payments.
EncryptionConverting data into code to prevent unauthorized access during transmission.
Two-Factor AuthenticationExtra security requiring two forms of verification to access accounts.
Background: Online shopping offers unmatched convenience—comparing prices, reading reviews, and shopping 24/7 from anywhere. But this convenience comes with risks: scam websites, identity theft, and lost packages. The key to safe online shopping is verification: checking that websites are secure (HTTPS), researching unfamiliar retailers, using protected payment methods (credit cards over debit), and trusting your instincts when deals seem too good to be true.
