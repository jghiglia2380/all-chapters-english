Pre-Investment Checklist

1. Core Portfolio First
I have a solid foundation of low-cost index funds (stocks/bonds) before considering alternatives
2. Emergency Fund Complete
I have 3-6 months of expenses saved in liquid, accessible accounts
3. Understand What I'm Buying
I can explain this investment to someone else in plain language
4. Know the Risks
I understand and accept the specific risks (illiquidity, volatility, total loss potential)
5. Fees Are Transparent
I know all fees including management, performance, and hidden costs

Due Diligence Checklist

6. Reviewed Historical Performance
I've looked at 5+ years of returns and understand they don't guarantee future results
7. Checked for Correlation
This investment adds diversification (low correlation with my existing holdings)
8. Verified the Manager/Platform
I've researched the fund manager or platform's track record and reputation
9. Understand Liquidity Terms
I know when and how I can access my money (lock-up periods, redemption windows)
10. Tax Implications Clear
I understand how this investment will be taxed (K-1s, ordinary income, capital gains)

Position Sizing Rules

11. Maximum Allocation Limit
Total alternatives won't exceed my predetermined percentage of portfolio
12. Single Position Limit
No single alternative investment will exceed 5% of my total portfolio
13. "Sleep Test"
If this investment went to zero, I could still sleep at night
14. Time Horizon Match
My investment timeline matches the illiquidity period of this investment
15. No FOMO Decisions
I'm not investing because others are - I've done my own analysis
