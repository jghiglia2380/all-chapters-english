Financial Service Providers Reference Sheet

Quick Guide to Choosing and Working with Financial Institutions

Types of Financial Service Providers

  • Traditional Banks: Full-service institutions with physical branches, ATMs, and complete product offerings. FDIC insured up to $250,000.
  • Credit Unions: Member-owned, not-for-profit cooperatives offering lower fees and better rates. NCUA insured up to $250,000.
  • Online Banks: Digital-only institutions with higher interest rates and lower fees due to reduced overhead costs.
  • Brokerages: Investment-focused firms that may offer cash management accounts with banking features.
Key Services Offered
  • Checking accounts - daily transactions
  • Savings accounts - emergency funds
  • Certificates of Deposit (CDs) - fixed-term savings
  • Money market accounts - higher-yield savings
  • Credit cards - revolving credit
  • Personal loans - fixed borrowing
  • Auto loans - vehicle financing
  • Mortgages - home financing
  • Investment services - wealth building
Fees to Watch For
  • Monthly maintenance fees - $5-15/month
  • ATM fees - $2-5 per transaction
  • Overdraft fees - $25-35 per occurrence
  • Wire transfer fees - $15-50
  • Foreign transaction fees - 1-3%
  • Account closure fees - $25-50
  • Paper statement fees - $2-5/month
  • Minimum balance fees - varies
  • Returned check fees - $25-40

How to Choose a Provider
  • Identify your primary banking needs
  • Compare fees across multiple providers
  • Check interest rates for accounts
  • Evaluate convenience (branches, ATMs)
  • Review mobile app ratings and features
  • Verify insurance coverage (FDIC/NCUA)
  • Read customer service reviews
  • Consider bundling opportunities
Red Flags to Avoid
  • Unusually high or hidden fees
  • Poor online/mobile capabilities
  • Consistently negative reviews
  • Lack of FDIC/NCUA insurance
  • Pressure to open unnecessary accounts
  • Unclear fee disclosures
  • Limited customer service hours
  • No fraud protection policies
Essential Questions to Ask Providers
Fees: "What fees are associated with this account and how can I avoid them?"
Interest: "What is the current APY and how often does it change?"
Access: "How many free ATMs are available and what are out-of-network fees?"
Minimums: "What are the minimum balance requirements to avoid fees?"
Overdraft: "What overdraft protection options do you offer and what do they cost?"
Security: "What fraud protection and security features are included?"
Mobile: "What can I do through your mobile app? Is mobile deposit available?"
Support: "What are your customer service hours and contact options?"
