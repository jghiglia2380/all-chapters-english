Part 2: Consumer Surplus Scenarios

Instructions: For each scenario, analyze the market structure and how it affects your consumer surplus (the difference between what you're willing to pay and what you actually pay).

Scenario A: Internet Service

You need internet service for your apartment. There's only one provider in your area charging $80/month. You value internet at $100/month.

Scenario B: Smartphone Purchase

You're buying a new smartphone. Three major brands (Apple, Samsung, Google) dominate 90% of the market. You value a good phone at $900, and prices range from $700-$1200.

Scenario C: Generic Medication

You need ibuprofen for headaches. Store brand costs $4, Advil costs $12. Both contain the exact same active ingredient in the same dosage.
