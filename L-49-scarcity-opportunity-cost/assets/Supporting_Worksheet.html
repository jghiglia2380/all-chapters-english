Opportunity Cost Practice Worksheet

Analyze real-world decisions using opportunity cost thinking

Part 1: Identifying Opportunity Costs
Scenario 1: Part-Time Job Decision

You're offered a part-time job that pays $15/hour for 15 hours per week. However, accepting it means you'll have less time for studying and extracurricular activities.

a) What is the monetary benefit of taking this job?
b) What are THREE opportunity costs of taking this job?
c) How would you make this decision using opportunity cost analysis?
Hint: Consider the value of study time on your grades, extracurricular activities on college applications, and social time on your well-being.
Scenario 2: College Choice

You're deciding between State University (total cost: $15,000/year) and Private University (total cost: $40,000/year). Both offer similar programs.

a) What is the direct cost difference between these options?
b) What is the opportunity cost of choosing Private University?
c) What factors beyond direct costs should you consider?
Hint: Think about what you could do with the $25,000/year difference. Consider internships, networking opportunities, and program quality.
