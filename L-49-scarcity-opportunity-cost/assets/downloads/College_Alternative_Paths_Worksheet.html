Key Insight

Over 10 years, Path B (Trade School) shows the highest total earnings in this scenario. However, Path A (University) may have better long-term growth potential beyond year 10, with higher salary ceilings and more career flexibility. The "best" choice depends on individual goals, interests, and circumstances.

Discussion Questions

1. Which path has the lowest opportunity cost in the first 4 years? Why?

2. Which path results in the highest total wealth after 10 years?

3. What factors beyond money should Alex consider (job satisfaction, physical demands, flexibility, growth potential)?

4. How would your analysis change if university tuition doubled? If trade school apprenticeship paid $45,000/year?

5. How does this analysis apply to YOUR post-high school decision?
