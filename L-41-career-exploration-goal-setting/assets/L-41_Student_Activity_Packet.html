Chapter 15.1: Career Exploration and Goal Setting

Student Activity Packet
The Challenge

Miguel was starting his junior year unsure about what career to pursue. He knew he loved technology and helping people, but couldn't figure out how to combine these interests into a career. After conducting a self-assessment, researching options, and talking to professionals, he discovered UX/UI design—a field that perfectly blended his technical skills with his desire to make technology more user-friendly. He set SMART goals, created a plan, and secured his first design internship by summer.

How can a structured approach to career exploration help you identify opportunities you might not have considered and create a clear path to achieving your professional goals?

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the difference between a job and a career.
  • Identify personal interests, skills, and values for career exploration.
  • Research career paths using appropriate resources.
  • Develop SMART career goals for short-term and long-term planning.
  • Create a personalized career development plan.
Core Concepts
Term Definition
Career A long-term professional journey that may encompass multiple jobs, involving growth, development, and advancement over time.
Job A specific position or role with defined responsibilities, typically performed in exchange for payment.
Self-Assessment The process of evaluating your own interests, skills, values, and personality traits to inform career decisions.
SMART Goals Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound objectives for career planning.
Career Development Plan A strategic roadmap outlining steps to achieve your professional goals.
Background: Understanding the distinction between jobs and careers is fundamental to effective career planning. While jobs provide immediate income, careers offer long-term growth and fulfillment aligned with your personal interests and values. A structured approach to career exploration—involving self-assessment, research, goal setting, and planning—helps ensure your professional path aligns with both your personal aspirations and financial goals.
