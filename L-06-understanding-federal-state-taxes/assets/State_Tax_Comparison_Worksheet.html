Comparing State Tax Structures

This worksheet will guide you through researching and comparing the tax structures of three different states. Understanding how different states approach taxation will help you analyze how geographic location affects overall tax burden and can influence financial decisions.

Instructions: Select three states to research, one from each of these categories:

  • No Income Tax State: Alaska, Florida, Nevada, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, or Wyoming
  • Flat Tax Rate State: Colorado, Illinois, Indiana, Kentucky, Massachusetts, Michigan, North Carolina, Pennsylvania, or Utah
  • Progressive Tax Brackets State: Arkansas, California, Delaware, Georgia, Hawaii, Idaho, Iowa, Kansas, Louisiana, Maine, Maryland, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri, Montana, Nebraska, New Jersey, New Mexico, New York, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Oregon, Rhode Island, South Carolina, Vermont, Virginia, West Virginia, or Wisconsin

Helpful Research Resources:

  • Tax Foundation State Tax Resource Center
  • Sales Tax Institute - State Sales Tax Rates
  • Property Tax Rates by State
  • Each state's Department of Revenue website
