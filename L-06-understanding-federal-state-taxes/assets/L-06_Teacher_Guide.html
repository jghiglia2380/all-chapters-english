Answer Key
Part A: Tax Type Classification
1. Federal Income Tax: PROGRESSIVE. Higher earners pay higher percentages due to increasing tax brackets.
2. Sales Tax: REGRESSIVE. Everyone pays the same rate, but lower-income people spend a higher percentage of their income on taxable goods.
3. Social Security Tax: REGRESSIVE (technically). While proportional up to the wage cap (~$160,000), income above the cap isn't taxed, so high earners pay a lower overall percentage.
Part B: State Tax Comparison
California: $6,000 + $2,400 + $4,590 = $12,990 total taxes Take-home: $60,000 - $12,990 = $47,010 Texas: $6,000 + $0 + $4,590 = $10,590 total taxes Take-home: $60,000 - $10,590 = $49,410
4. Texas resident has $2,400 more take-home pay. This could be offset by higher property taxes (Texas has ~1.8% vs California's ~0.77%), higher sales tax in some Texas cities, or lack of certain state services.

Common Misconceptions
MisconceptionClarification
"Moving to a higher tax bracket means ALL my income is taxed at that rate."Only the income WITHIN each bracket is taxed at that rate. If you earn $50,000, only $5,275 is taxed at 22%—not all $50,000.
"States with no income tax are always cheaper."No-income-tax states often have higher property taxes, sales taxes, or fees. Total tax burden depends on individual circumstances.
"FICA taxes are optional."FICA (Social Security + Medicare) is mandatory for all wage earners. It funds programs you'll likely use in retirement.
