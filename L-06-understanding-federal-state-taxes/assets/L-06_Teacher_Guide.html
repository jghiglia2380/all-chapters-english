Part A: Tax Type Classification
1. Federal Income Tax: PROGRESSIVE. Higher earners pay higher percentages due to increasing tax brackets.
2. Sales Tax ({{STATE_SALES_TAX}}% in {{STATE_NAME}}): REGRESSIVE. Everyone pays the same rate, but lower-income people spend a higher percentage of their income on taxable goods.
3. Social Security Tax: REGRESSIVE (technically). While proportional up to the wage cap (~$160,000), income above the cap isn't taxed, so high earners pay a lower overall percentage.

Common Misconceptions
MisconceptionClarification
"Moving to a higher tax bracket means ALL my income is taxed at that rate."Only the income WITHIN each bracket is taxed at that rate. If you earn $50,000, only $5,275 is taxed at 22%—not all $50,000.
"States with no income tax are always cheaper than {{STATE_NAME}}."No-income-tax states often have higher property taxes, sales taxes, or fees. Total tax burden depends on individual circumstances. Compare {{STATE_NAME}}'s {{STATE_PROPERTY_TAX_AVG}}% property tax to Texas's ~1.8%.
"FICA taxes are optional."FICA (Social Security + Medicare) is mandatory for all wage earners. It funds programs you'll likely use in retirement.
